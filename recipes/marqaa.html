Description

Marqaa is a traditional, thick Oromo porridge

made from roasted barley flour, water, and salt—that serves as a staple nutritious breakfast and a celebrated meal for special occasions like births and weddings.

It is traditionally served in a bowl with a central well filled with melted, spiced butter (dhadhaa) and yogur

Key Aspects of Oromo Marqaa:

  • Preparation: Flour (barley is most traditional, sometimes wheat) is stirred into boiling water until extremely thick and smooth
  • Serving style It is often served hot with a "well" made in the center for clarified butter andberbere (chili powder) or yogurt
  • Cultural Significance: As a traditional meal from the Oromia regional state of Ethiopia.
